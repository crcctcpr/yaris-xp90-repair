- Toyota recommends 5W-30 for ALL markets and climates

- Do NOT use 10W-30 or 10W-40 on the 1KR-FE engine
- Overfilling by more than 0.5L can cause seal leakage and oil foaming

| **Change Interval** | Every 100,000 km or 5 years | Whichever comes first |
| **Cost per Liter** | $2–$8 (concentrate); $3–$10 (pre-mixed) | – |

### Notes
- **DO NOT MIX with older red or orange coolants** — different inhibitor packages can cause sludge and corrosion
- Distilled water is preferred for mixing (tap water contains minerals that contribute to rust)
- If pre-mixed coolant is used, do NOT dilute further — it is already 50/50
- Coolant is toxic — never dump used coolant down the drain; recycle at auto parts stores or hazmat facilities
- **Never use silicate-free coolant without OAT inhibitors** for this engine

## TRANSMISSION FLUID (AUTOMATIC)

| Specification | Detail | Part Number / Alternative |
|---------------|--------|--------------------------|
| **Type** | Toyota WS (World Standard) Automatic Transmission Fluid | – |
| **OEM Product** | Toyota Genuine ATF WS | Toyota 08886-80506 (1L) |
| **Capacity (U340E)** | 3.7 liters (drain and refill with pan removal); 5.5–6.0 liters (full system including torque converter) | – |
| **Alternative Brands** | Mobil 1 Automatic Transmission Fluid, Red Line, Amsoil ATF, Castrol ATF | WS equivalent or "multi-purpose ATF" |
| **Color** | Red (correct color; brown = overheated or contaminated) | – |
| **Change Interval** | Every 80,000–100,000 km (high-performance use); every 40,000 km (city driving or towing) | – |
| **Cost per Liter** | $8–$18 | – |

### Notes
- **Use ONLY Toyota WS ATF** — mixing with other brands or Dexron III can cause shifting issues and seal problems
- If older red ATF was used, flush the system completely before using new WS fluid
- Drain-and-refill only replaces ~50% of fluid (torque converter retains ~50%)
- A professional flush service is recommended every 80,000–100,000 km for maximum transmission life
- Never overfill — check with engine running, in Park, on level ground

## BRAKE FLUID

| Specification | Detail | Part Number / Alternative |
|---------------|--------|--------------------------|
| **Type** | DOT 3 or DOT 4 (check fuse box lid or door jamb) | – |
| **Boiling Point** | DOT 3: 205°C; DOT 4: 230°C (dry) | – |
| **OEM Product** | Toyota Genuine Brake Fluid DOT 3 | Toyota 08889-00077 (1L) |
| **Capacity (Full System)** | 0.7–1.0 liters (reservoir + lines + calipers/cylinders) | – |
| **Alternative Brands** | Motul DOT 3/4, Bosch DOT 3/4, ATE SuperBlue, Castrol DOT 4 | Equivalent DOT rating |
| **Change Interval** | Every 2 years or 40,000 km (absorbs moisture over time) | – |
| **Cost per Bottle** | $5–$15 (500 mL bottle) | – |

### Notes
- **DO NOT MIX DOT 3 and DOT 4** — different viscosity characteristics can cause brake feel problems
- **DO NOT use DOT 5 (silicone)** — not compatible with Yaris rubber seals
- Brake fluid is hygroscopic (absorbs water) — do NOT leave the reservoir cap off
- Old brake fluid turns dark brown/black — replace during regular maintenance
- Keep unused fluid sealed; once opened, use within one season

## REFRIGERANT (AIR CONDITIONING)

| Specification | Detail | Notes |
|---------------|--------|-------|
| **Type** | R134a (hydrofluoroalkane) | – |
| **Charge Amount** | Approximately 400–500 grams (varies by system) | Consult A/C sticker under hood |
| **Oil Type** | PAO (polyalphaolefin) or ester-based A/C oil | Use with R134a ONLY |
| **System Pressure** | High side: ~20–30 psi (idle); Low side: ~35–45 psi | Pressures vary with ambient temperature |

### Notes
- **DO NOT use R12 (CFC)** — it is banned; incompatible oils cause corrosion
- R134a is not ozone-destructive but is a greenhouse gas — handle with care
- A/C recharge is best done by professionals with evacuation and moisture testing equipment
- Refrigerant should NEVER be vented to atmosphere; recovery equipment is mandatory

| **Cost** | $80–$200 | Price varies by brand and capacity |

### Notes
- The Yaris does NOT require a BMS (Battery Management System) registration after replacement — simply swap and go
- AGM (Absorbent Glass Mat) batteries are more expensive but last longer and tolerate deep discharge better
- Ensure correct polarity when installing: **RED to POSITIVE terminal, BLACK to NEGATIVE (ground)**
