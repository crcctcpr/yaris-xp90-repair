# Complete Torque Specifications — 2011 Toyota Yaris XP90

> **CRITICAL:** Always use a calibrated torque wrench. Over-tightening can strip threads or break bolts. Under-tightening can cause fastener loosening and failure.
> When a bolt is torqued and then requires an angular rotation (e.g., "36 Nm + 90°"), torque to the specified Nm first, THEN rotate the bolt head by the specified angle.

## GENERAL NOTES

### When to Use Thread-Locking Compound
- **Small fasteners** (brake bleeder screw): Use medium-strength Loctite (Loctite 243)
- **Critical fasteners** (suspension points, brake lines): Consider medium-strength Loctite
- **Engine fasteners**: Generally use OEM torque spec without thread locker unless specifically noted

### Bolt Grade Recognition
- **Grade 5 (most common):** Plain finish with radial lines on head
- **Grade 8 (higher strength):** Radial lines + number "8" on head
- Higher grade bolts require higher torque — always verify the bolt grade before applying torque

### Torque Sequence Terminology
- **Criss-cross pattern:** Tighten bolts in an "X" pattern (diagonal), not in a line
- **Star pattern:** Tighten in a star shape from center outward (prevents warping in flat surfaces like transmission pans)
- **Sequence specified in manual:** Always follow the exact sequence provided in the Toyota factory manual for critical components (cylinder head, engine block)
