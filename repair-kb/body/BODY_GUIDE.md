# Body & Interior Guide — 2011 Toyota Yaris XP90

---

## 1. FRONT DOOR PANEL REMOVAL

### Tools Required
- Plastic trim removal tools (set of 3–4, essential — metal tools can scratch plastic)
- T20 or T25 Torx screwdriver (door panel fasteners vary by market)
- Small flathead screwdriver (to pry clips)

### Procedure

1. **Remove the manual window switch (if equipped) or power window switch panel:**
   - Locate the window switch pod on the door armrest area (lower left side of the door, within reach of the driver)
   - If manual crank: unscrew the handle (typically held by a set screw or clip)
   - If power buttons: this panel typically has 2–3 clips holding it in place from the side and bottom — pry gently with a plastic trim tool
   - Disconnect the electrical connector (push-tab)

2. **Remove the door handle bezel (trim ring around the door pull handle):**
   - The bezel is held in place by clips — pry gently from the bottom or outer edge with a plastic trim tool
   - Once the bezel is loose, pull it away from the door — be careful not to pull any connected wires

3. **Remove the door pull handle screws:**
   - Behind the bezel are typically 2–3 screws (T20 or Phillips head) — remove these
   - The handle will now be loose but may still be connected by a wire for the interior lights (disconnect if present)

4. **Remove the mirror triangular trim panel:**
   - Located at the corner of the door near the side mirror — this panel has clips (no screws)
   - Pry gently from the bottom with a plastic trim tool
   - Once popped out, carefully pull it away

5. **Remove the door panel perimeter clips:**
   - **Top edge:** 2–3 clips along the top of the panel where it meets the window frame
   - **Front edge (near door latch):** 1–2 clips securing the panel to the door frame
   - **Bottom edge:** 2–3 clips along the bottom of the panel
   - **Rear edge:** 1–2 clips on the back side of the panel (facing the rear door if applicable, or the quarter panel)
   - Using plastic trim tools, carefully pop each clip starting from the bottom and working up — the panel is large and will want to fall, so have a helper or support it

6. **Disconnect remaining electrical connectors:**
   - Power window switch connector (already done if you removed it)
   - Door lock actuator connector (if equipped, electrical locks)
   - Interior light connector (if any)

7. **Lift the door panel away from the door frame:**
   - Have a helper support the panel or rest it on your lap (it's approximately 3–5 kg and awkward)
   - Set it aside on a safe surface (lean it against a wall, don't lay it face-down)

### Accessing the Door Interior

Once the panel is removed, you can access:
- **Window regulator mechanism** (see section 2)
- **Door lock actuator** (see ELECTRICAL_GUIDE.md)
- **Vapor barrier** (plastic sheet behind the panel) — can be removed for deeper work

## 2. WINDOW REGULATOR REPLACEMENT

### Symptoms of Failed Regulator
- Window won't go up or down
- Window moves very slowly
- Window falls down into the door (regulator fully failed)
- Grinding or clicking noise when window button is pressed

### Access

1. **Remove the door panel** (see section 1 above).
2. **Identify the regulator assembly:**
   - The regulator is a mechanical linkage (scissor-type on many Yaris models) with a motor bolted to one side
   - It's mounted inside the door cavity, accessible through the large opening where the panel was

3. **If the window has fallen:**
   - Locate the window glass at the bottom of the door cavity
   - The glass should be supported by the lower clips of the regulator
   - Gently support the window with a helper (or tape to the door frame with painter's tape from above)

### Removal

1. **Disconnect the window motor electrical connector** — push-tab.
2. **Lower the window partially** — use a 12V power source connected to the motor to lower the window approximately 1/3 of the way down (makes removal easier).
3. **Disconnect the window glass from the regulator:**
   - At the bottom of the window glass, there are typically 2 clips or brackets where the glass sits on the regulator track
   - These clips are held in place by bolts or rivets (10mm bolts if rivets were replaced)
   - Remove the fasteners and lift the glass up and away
4. **Remove regulator mounting bolts:**
   - The regulator is typically held to the door by 3–4 bolts (10mm) along its length
   - Remove each bolt — the regulator will now be free
5. **If the motor is integral (can't be separated):** Remove the regulator/motor as one assembly.
6. **If the motor is separate:** Unbolt the motor (3 bolts) from the regulator arm, and the motor will come free.

### Installation

1. **Position the new regulator in the door cavity** — it should slide into the same mounting points as the old one.
2. **Install mounting bolts** — torque to approximately 10 Nm (snug, not tight).
3. **Connect the electrical connector** to the window motor.
4. **Raise the window** to approximately 1/3 travel using 12V power (test the motor before fully securing).
5. **Install the window glass** onto the regulator clips:
   - Lower the window so the glass seats into the regulator bracket
   - Ensure the glass is centered and not binding
   - Install fasteners (bolts or new rivets if original holes are enlarged)
6. **Test the window:**
   - Raise the window fully — it should move smoothly without grinding or binding
   - Lower the window fully — it should rest firmly against the bottom seal
   - If binding: adjust the regulator position slightly or re-seat the glass
7. **Reinstall the door panel** (see section 1, reverse order).

**Window Regulator/Motor Part:** OEM $80–$180; Aftermarket $30–$80

## 3. DOOR MIRROR REMOVAL & REPLACEMENT

### Mirror Type
The driver's side mirror on the XP90 typically has:
- Manual or electric adjustment (rocker switch inside door)
- Heated mirror (if equipped)
- Power folding (if equipped)

### Procedure

1. **From outside the car, gently pry the mirror trim bezel:**
   - The trim ring holding the mirror glass to the mirror housing has a small gap
   - Use a plastic pry tool to pop the bezel off
   - The glass will come free with the bezel

2. **Disconnect the mirror glass wiring (if heated or power-folding):**
   - Carefully peel back the bezel — there's a small connector for the heater/fold motor
   - Push-tab or spade connectors — disconnect

3. **Access the mirror mounting bolts from INSIDE the door:**
   - Remove the door panel (see section 1) or at minimum, peel back the vapor barrier in the area behind the mirror mounting point
   - There are typically **3 bolts** (12mm or 10mm) securing the mirror housing to the door — they are located on the inside of the door frame where the mirror connects
   - Remove these bolts — the mirror will now be loose from outside

4. **From outside, pull the mirror housing straight outward:**
   - The mirror will come free along with any remaining wiring
   - Disconnect any remaining connectors (power fold, if equipped)

5. **Installation is reverse:**
   - Position new mirror housing and install 3 bolts from inside
   - Torque to approximately 20 Nm
   - Reconnect all wiring
   - If equipped, press the mirror glass and bezel back onto the housing
   - Re-connect heater/power fold wiring

**Door Mirror Part:** OEM $60–$150; Aftermarket $30–$80

## 4. FRONT BUMPER REMOVAL

### Procedure

1. **Open the hood and locate the 4 bolts at the top of the bumper:**
   - 2 bolts on the driver's side (above the headlight area)
   - 2 bolts on the passenger's side
   - These are typically 10mm bolts securing the bumper shell to the impact absorber bar

2. **Remove the 4 top bolts** (10mm socket).

3. **Locate the bottom side-mounting bolts:**
   - These are typically behind plastic trim panels under the headlights and near the fog light (if equipped)
   - Remove the trim pieces to access the bolts (usually clips and a few screws)
   - Remove 2 bottom bolts (1 per side, near the wheel wells)

4. **Disconnect any wiring (if equipped with fog lights or sensors):**
   - Fog light connectors (if equipped) — push-tab
   - Parking sensor connectors (if equipped) — separate as needed

5. **Carefully pull the bumper straight forward and away from the car:**
   - Have a helper support the bumper — it's approximately 5–8 kg and awkward
   - Once clear of the mounting points, set it down on a clean surface (lean against a wall, face-up)

6. **To reinstall:**
   - Reconnect all wiring
   - Align the bumper with the mounting points
   - Install all bolts (top 4, bottom 2) — torque to approximately 10 Nm
   - Reinstall trim panels
   - Close hood and test lights/sensors

## 5. AIR CONDITIONING SYSTEM

### System Overview
- **Refrigerant:** R134a (do NOT use R12 — incompatible with current oils)
- **Compressor:** Driven by the accessory drive belt
- **Cabin filter:** Located behind the glove box, should be replaced every 15,000–20,000 km

### Cabin Filter Replacement

1. **Open the glove box and remove items.**
2. **Locate the cabin filter housing** (behind the glove box, passenger side):
   - The housing is a black plastic box with a removable cover
   - There are typically 2 clips or bolts holding the cover
3. **Remove the old cabin filter:**
   - It may be stuck — gently pull
   - Note the orientation (arrows show airflow direction)
4. **Install the new filter:**
   - Insert with the same orientation (typically airflow arrows point downward into the cabin)
   - Ensure the filter seats fully in the housing
5. **Reinstall the cover** and restore the glove box.

**Cabin Filter Part:** Toyota Part: 87139-XX150 (varies) — $15–$30

### Compressor Clutch Test

If the A/C is not blowing cold but the compressor is not engaging:

1. Locate the A/C compressor clutch (the pulley on the front of the compressor).
2. With the A/C turned ON:
   - **Clutch engaged:** The pulley center will spin with the compressor shaft — you should see NO gap between the pulley and compressor body
   - **Clutch disengaged:** The pulley spins freely but the compressor shaft is stationary — visible gap
3. **If the clutch won't engage:**
   - Check the A/C clutch relay (engine bay fuse box)
   - Test for 12V signal at the compressor clutch coil (small connector on the compressor)
   - If no signal: relay or electrical issue
   - If signal is present but clutch doesn't engage: compressor coil is failed — compressor replacement needed

### A/C Recharge Overview

Refrigerant recharge is possible as a DIY task but requires:
- Refrigerant tank (R134a)
- Charging hose and gauge set
- Safety gear (gloves, eye protection)

**Professional A/C service is recommended** for:
- Leak diagnosis and repair (most A/C problems are leaks, not just low charge)
- System evacuation (removes old refrigerant and moisture) before recharging
- Moisture testing (water in the system causes compressor damage)

**Professional A/C recharge cost:** $100–$200 (vacuum + evacuation + recharge)

## 6. COMMON WATER LEAK POINTS & WEATHERSTRIP REPLACEMENT

### Common Leak Sources

| Location | Cause | Fix |
|----------|-------|-----|
| Door windows | Weatherstripping dried/cracked | Replace weatherstrip |
| Door seals | Worn rubber gasket | Replace door gasket |
| Windshield | Degraded sealant | Re-seal or replace windshield |
| Sunroof drain tubes | Clogged drain lines | Flush lines with water |
| A/C evaporator drain | Blocked drain hole under car | Poke clear with wire or hose |
| Rear hatch gasket | Compressed/cracked | Replace gasket |
| Hood seals | Worn rubber | Replace hood gasket |

### Weatherstrip Replacement Procedure (Door Windows)

1. **Remove the door panel** (see section 1).
2. **Locate the weatherstrip** (rubber gasket running along the window frame inside the door):
   - It's glued in place with contact cement
   - Carefully peel it away — use a heat gun (hair dryer) to soften the adhesive
   - Once removed, clean the frame surface of old adhesive with rubbing alcohol and a cloth
3. **Prepare the new weatherstrip:**
   - If it has adhesive backing: remove the plastic protector
   - If it comes dry: apply contact cement to both the frame and the strip
4. **Press the new strip in place:**
   - Start at one end and work slowly, pressing firmly as you go
   - Ensure it seals all gaps and no air pockets
5. **Allow adhesive to cure** (typically 24 hours before rolling windows up/down).

**Weatherstrip Kit (4 doors):** $30–$80 per set
