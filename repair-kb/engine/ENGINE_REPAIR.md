# Engine Repair Guide — 2011 Toyota Yaris XP90
## Engines Covered: 1KR-FE (1.0L 3-cyl), 2NZ-FE (1.3L 4-cyl), 1NR-FE (1.3L 4-cyl)

---

## 1. ENGINE OIL CHANGE (1KR-FE)

### Specifications
- **Capacity:** 3.0L (with filter)
- **Viscosity:** 5W-30 (Toyota Genuine Motor Oil or equivalent)
- **Drain plug torque:** 37 Nm
- **Filter location:** Passenger side of engine, lower area

### Procedure
1. Warm the engine for 5 minutes, then shut off.
2. Raise and support the vehicle safely on jack stands.
3. Place drain pan under the oil drain plug (14mm bolt, lower rear of oil pan).
4. Remove drain plug — allow full drainage (3–5 minutes).
5. Remove the oil filter (located on passenger side of block, lower). Use a filter wrench if needed.
6. Lightly coat the new filter's O-ring with fresh oil.
7. Thread new filter on by hand until O-ring contacts, then 3/4 turn further.
8. Reinstall drain plug with new crush washer — torque to **37 Nm**.
9. Fill with 2.7L of 5W-30 from the top, then start engine briefly, shut off, wait 1 minute, check dipstick.
10. Add remaining oil to reach FULL mark. Total capacity 3.0L with filter change.
11. Check for leaks at drain plug and filter.

## 2. AIR FILTER REPLACEMENT (1KR-FE)

### Location
Airbox on driver's side of engine bay, connected to intake tube leading to throttle body.

### Procedure
1. Loosen the hose clamp on the intake tube at the airbox outlet.
2. Unclip the 4 spring clips (or unscrew 4 screws, depending on variant) holding the airbox lid.
3. Lift the lid and remove the old paper air filter element.
4. Inspect the airbox interior — wipe out with a clean rag if dusty.
5. Install new filter element (Toyota Part: 17801-40020 or NGK/Bosch equivalent).
6. Reseat airbox lid and reclip/rescrew all fasteners.
7. Reconnect intake tube and tighten hose clamp.

**Interval:** Every 30,000 km or 24 months under normal conditions. Every 15,000 km in dusty environments.

## 3. SPARK PLUG REPLACEMENT (1KR-FE)

### Specifications
- **Plug:** NGK ILKAR7L11 (Iridium laser)
- **Gap:** 1.0–1.1 mm (do not adjust iridium plugs — they are pre-gapped)
- **Torque:** 18 Nm
- **Thread size:** M14 × 1.25
- **Plug count:** 3 (one per cylinder)

### Tools Required
- 16mm spark plug socket with rubber insert
- Torque wrench
- Extension bar (150mm)
- Ratchet

### Procedure
1. Remove the engine cover if fitted (plastic, 2–3 push clips).
2. Disconnect the ignition coil connectors (1 per cylinder, push-tab type).
3. Remove the ignition coil hold-down bolt (10mm) on each coil.
4. Wiggle each coil straight up to remove — do not pull on the wiring.
5. With the 16mm plug socket + extension, loosen each spark plug counterclockwise.
6. Remove plugs and inspect:
   - Normal: light tan/gray electrode
   - Rich: sooty black
   - Lean/overheating: white/blistered
   - Oil fouling: oily black
7. Check gap on new NGK ILKAR7L11 — factory gap 1.0 mm, acceptable 1.0–1.1 mm. **Do NOT adjust iridium electrode.**
8. Thread new plugs in by hand to prevent cross-threading.
9. Torque to **18 Nm**.
10. Reinstall coils — press firmly until they click/seat, then install hold-down bolt (snug, ~10 Nm).
11. Reconnect coil connectors.
12. Clear any misfire codes with OBD-II scanner.

**Interval:** 100,000 km for iridium plugs.

## 4. VALVE COVER GASKET REPLACEMENT (1KR-FE)

### Symptoms of Failure
- Oil smell from engine bay
- Oil visible on top of engine / around plug wells
- Oil fouled spark plugs
- Misfire codes (P0301–P0303) if oil enters plug wells

### Parts Required
- Valve cover gasket set (includes cam seal and plug tube seals)
- Toyota Part: 11213-40010 (gasket), 11189-28010 (plug tube seals ×3)

### Procedure
1. Allow engine to cool completely.
2. Remove engine cover.
3. Disconnect all 3 ignition coil connectors and remove coils (see spark plug section).
4. Remove spark plugs (optional but recommended to prevent debris falling in).
5. Unclip the PCV hose from the valve cover.
6. Unclip any wiring harness routed across the valve cover.
7. Remove valve cover bolts (8mm, typically 8–10 bolts) in a criss-cross pattern from outside inward.
8. Lift valve cover — if stuck, tap gently with rubber mallet. Do NOT pry.
9. Clean mating surfaces:
   - Remove old gasket material from head and cover with plastic scraper
   - Clean with brake cleaner on a rag
   - Ensure surfaces are completely dry
10. Install new plug tube seals — press into valve cover firmly by hand.
11. Install new valve cover gasket in the groove of the valve cover.
12. Apply a small dab of Toyota Seal Packing (black RTV, Part: 08826-00080) at the two cam cap corners where the head casting meets the cam bearing caps — this prevents leaks at these corner points.
13. Carefully lower valve cover onto head, ensuring gasket stays seated.
14. Thread all bolts by hand.
15. Torque valve cover bolts to **8 Nm** in criss-cross sequence from center outward.
16. Reinstall PCV hose, ignition coils, spark plugs, wiring.
17. Start engine and check for leaks immediately.

## 5. THROTTLE BODY CLEANING (1KR-FE)

### When Needed
- Rough idle / hunting idle
- Hesitation on acceleration
- P0505, P0506, P0507 codes

### Procedure
1. Allow engine to cool.
2. Disconnect the negative battery terminal (wait 10 seconds).
3. Loosen the intake tube hose clamp at the throttle body and disconnect.
4. Unplug the Throttle Position Sensor (TPS) / Electronic Throttle Control (ETC) connector.
5. Remove 4 bolts (10mm) holding the throttle body to the intake manifold.
6. Remove throttle body — note the gasket/O-ring.
7. With a clean rag and throttle body cleaner spray:
   - Open the throttle plate manually by pressing the blade
   - Spray cleaner inside the bore and on the back of the plate
   - Wipe clean with rag — repeat until no brown deposits remain
8. **Do NOT spray MAF sensor cleaner or get cleaner on the TPS itself.**
9. Inspect the gasket/O-ring — replace if flattened or cracked.
10. Reinstall throttle body, torque bolts to 10 Nm.
11. Reconnect intake tube, TPS connector, battery negative.
12. Perform throttle position relearn:
    - Turn ignition ON (do not start), wait 15 seconds
    - Turn ignition OFF, wait 15 seconds
    - Start engine and allow to idle for 3 minutes
    - Idle should stabilize — if not, perform Toyota idle relearn with a scanner

## 6. COMPRESSION TEST (1KR-FE)

### Specifications
- **Normal compression:** 150–180 psi (10.3–12.4 bar)
- **Minimum acceptable:** 130 psi (9.0 bar)
- **Maximum variation between cylinders:** 14 psi (1.0 bar)

### Procedure
1. Warm engine to operating temperature, then shut off.
2. Disable the ignition system:
   - Disconnect all 3 ignition coil connectors
   - Or remove the EFI/IGN fuse
3. Remove all 3 spark plugs.
4. Thread compression gauge into cylinder 1 plug hole.
5. Have an assistant crank the engine for 5 seconds (or until gauge stabilizes — usually 4–6 compression strokes).
6. Record reading. Repeat for cylinders 2 and 3.
7. If any cylinder is below 130 psi or variation exceeds 14 psi between cylinders:
   - **Wet test:** Add ~15ml of engine oil into the low cylinder through plug hole
   - Retest: If compression rises significantly → worn piston rings
   - If compression stays low → valve leak (burned valve, bent valve, seat issue)
8. Low compression on adjacent cylinders (e.g., 1 & 2) often indicates head gasket failure between those cylinders.

## 7. TIMING CHAIN INSPECTION & REPLACEMENT (1KR-FE)

> ⚠️ The 1KR-FE uses a **timing chain** (no scheduled replacement). However, chains can stretch with poor oil maintenance.

### Checking for Chain Stretch

#### Method 1: Scanner Check
- Connect OBD-II scanner
- Check cam timing deviation (live data)
- Values more than ±6° from specified VVT position at idle may indicate chain stretch or tensioner wear

#### Method 2: Physical Inspection
1. Remove valve cover (see section 4)
2. Rotate crankshaft by hand (via crank bolt, 19mm, clockwise only)
3. Align crank to TDC: Timing mark on crank pulley aligns with "0" on timing cover
4. With crank at TDC compression (cylinder 1), inspect cam sprocket marks:
   - Both cam sprocket timing marks should align with reference lines on the cam bearing caps or cylinder head surface
   - Exhaust cam: mark points toward 12 o'clock (or specified mark on head)
   - Intake cam: mark points toward 12 o'clock
5. If cam marks are off by more than 1 tooth, chain has stretched significantly

#### Method 3: Tensioner Test
1. With valve cover removed, locate the chain tensioner (right side of engine, behind timing cover on block)
2. Press the tensioner plunger with a screwdriver through access hole — it should resist firmly and spring back
3. If the plunger moves freely or does not return: tensioner is worn/failed

### Timing Chain Replacement Procedure (1KR-FE)

#### Special Tools Required
- Camshaft locking pins / cam lock tool set (Toyota SST 09213-70010 or equivalent)
- Crank holding tool or flywheel lock
- 19mm socket for crank bolt
- Torque wrench

#### Parts Required
- Timing chain (Toyota Part: 13506-40030)
- Timing chain tensioner (Toyota Part: 13540-40011)
- Chain tensioner slipper/guide (Toyota Part: 13560-40010)
- Chain vibration damper (Toyota Part: 13561-40010)
- Cam sprocket (VVT-i) if worn
- Timing cover gasket/seal set

#### Procedure
1. Disconnect battery negative terminal.
2. Remove the RH front wheel and inner fender liner for access.
3. Drain engine oil and coolant (the timing cover is part of the front engine cover and may require coolant system opening).
4. Remove accessory belt (tensioner pulley, 14mm bolt — slip belt off).
5. Remove alternator (2 bolts, disconnect wiring).
6. Remove the A/C compressor if equipped and set aside (do not disconnect refrigerant lines — just unbolt and hang).
7. Remove power steering pump if equipped.
8. Remove crank pulley bolt (19mm) — use flywheel lock or wedge tool in flywheel ring gear. **This bolt is very tight — typically 100–150 Nm.** Use an impact wrench or breaker bar.
9. Remove crank pulley/damper.
10. Remove the lower timing cover bolts. Note: some bolts also secure the oil pan — remove those as well.
11. Remove the oil pan (6–8 bolts, 10mm) — use RTV gasket cutter around perimeter. Have drain pan ready for remaining oil.
12. Remove the upper timing cover (cam cover area).
13. Remove all timing cover bolts and carefully pry the front cover off (RTV-sealed). Clean all mating surfaces.
14. **Set engine to TDC:**
    - Rotate crank until crank sprocket timing mark (single dot or punched mark) aligns with the "0" mark on the engine block (or oil pump body mark)
    - Both cam timing marks should be visible and aligned with marks on cam bearing caps
15. **Lock cams:** Insert cam locking pins into the camshaft timing pin holes (through the cam sprockets into holes in the cylinder head) — this holds cams at TDC
16. Lock the crankshaft (flywheel lock or helper holds crank from turning)
17. Remove the chain tensioner:
    - Insert a pin/drill bit through the tensioner access hole to lock the tensioner ratchet
    - Remove tensioner bolts and pull tensioner out
18. Slide the timing chain off the cam sprockets and crank sprocket.
19. Note condition of guide rails — if cracked or heavily worn, replace them now (they are cheap insurance).
20. Inspect all sprocket teeth for wear (hooking/pointing shape = replace).
21. Install new chain on crank sprocket with colored/marked link at crank timing mark.
22. Route chain up to intake cam sprocket (colored link at intake cam timing mark), then exhaust cam (colored link at exhaust timing mark).
23. Install new tensioner slipper and vibration damper.
24. Install new tensioner with pin retracted (pre-compressed with pin/wire).
25. Double-check all timing marks are aligned with chain installed.
26. Remove cam locking pins.
27. Remove tensioner lock pin — tensioner will load the chain.
28. Rotate engine 2 full turns by hand and recheck timing marks at TDC. Marks must realign correctly.
29. Apply Toyota Seal Packing (FIPG) to front cover mating surfaces per pattern.
30. Install front cover — all bolts finger tight, then torque in sequence.
31. Install oil pan with new RTV sealant.
32. Reinstall all accessories, crank pulley (new bolt or re-use per spec), alternator, belts.
33. Refill oil and coolant.
34. Start engine — should start within a few cranks. Listen for chain rattle; it should quiet after oil pressure builds.
35. Check for oil/coolant leaks.

## 8. VVT-i SOLENOID CLEANING AND REPLACEMENT (1KR-FE)

### Location
- The VVT-i Oil Control Valve (OCV) solenoid is located on the front of the cylinder head, next to the intake camshaft sprocket, at the top left of the engine as viewed from front.

### Electrical Specification
- **Resistance:** 6.9–7.9 Ω at 20°C
- If resistance is out of spec: replace solenoid

### Diagnosis
1. Connect OBD-II scanner — check for P0010, P0011, P0012, P1349
2. With engine warm and at idle, monitor VVT advance angle in live data (should vary as RPM changes and with VVT command)
3. Unplug solenoid connector and measure resistance across solenoid pins with multimeter
4. If resistance is 6.9–7.9 Ω: solenoid is electrically OK — check oil supply, oil viscosity, sludge
5. If resistance is out of range (open or short): replace solenoid
6. Check for 12V signal at connector during operation with DVOM

### Cleaning Procedure
1. Warm engine briefly to thin the oil, then shut off.
2. Disconnect negative battery terminal.
3. Locate solenoid (intake cam, top front of head — 1 bolt, 10mm, and electrical connector).
4. Disconnect the electrical connector.
5. Remove the single retaining bolt (10mm).
6. Pull solenoid out — some oil will drain, have rag ready.
7. Inspect the filter screen at the end of the solenoid — if clogged with sludge/debris:
   - Soak in mineral spirits or brake cleaner for 10–15 minutes
   - Use a small brush to clean the screen
   - Flush with cleaner and blow dry with compressed air
8. Inspect the O-ring — replace if worn or flat.
9. Reinstall solenoid — torque retaining bolt to **9 Nm**.
10. Reconnect electrical connector and battery.
11. Clear codes and test drive.

### Replacement Procedure
- Same as cleaning steps 1–6 above
- Install new solenoid, lightly oil the new O-ring
- Torque to **9 Nm**
- **Toyota Part:** 15330-40011 (OCV solenoid, 1KR-FE)
- **Aftermarket alternatives:** Aisin, Denso equivalents available ($40–$80 vs $120 OEM)

## 9. 2NZ-FE TIMING BELT — CRITICAL WARNING & REPLACEMENT

> ⚠️ **INTERFERENCE ENGINE WARNING**
> The 2NZ-FE is an **interference engine**. If the timing belt breaks while the engine is running, **piston-to-valve contact will occur**, resulting in **catastrophic engine damage** (bent valves, cracked pistons, possible head damage).

### Replacement Interval
- **Every 100,000 km** regardless of condition — or sooner if cracked, frayed, or glazed
- **Always replace the water pump at the same time** — the water pump is driven by the timing belt and failure of the pump will destroy the new belt

### Timing Mark Locations (2NZ-FE)
- **Crankshaft:** Timing mark on crank sprocket aligns with a notch/arrow on the oil pump body
- **Intake camshaft:** "I" mark or punch mark on cam sprocket aligns with mark on cam bearing cap surface
- **Exhaust camshaft:** "E" mark or punch mark on cam sprocket aligns with mark on cam bearing cap surface
- **Verify with workshop manual:** Mark positions can vary slightly by production year/market

### Parts Required
- Timing belt (Toyota Part: 13568-29405 or equivalent Gates/Dayco)
- Water pump + gasket (Toyota Part: 16100-29105 or Aisin WP-173)
- Timing belt tensioner
- Timing belt idler pulley (if worn)
- Cam seals (both cams) — replace while accessible
- Crank seal — replace while accessible
- Drive belt (if due)

### Timing Belt Replacement Procedure (2NZ-FE)

1. Disconnect battery negative terminal.
2. Remove RH front wheel and inner fender liner for access.
3. Drain coolant (water pump replacement requires this).
4. Remove the accessory drive belt.
5. Remove the crank pulley bolt (14mm or 19mm) — use flywheel lock.
6. Remove crank pulley / vibration damper.
7. Remove the upper and lower timing belt covers (4–6 bolts, 10mm).
8. **Set TDC:**
   - Rotate crankshaft clockwise until the crank sprocket mark aligns with the oil pump reference mark
   - Confirm both cam sprocket marks align with their reference marks on the cylinder head
   - If not on TDC compression stroke, rotate another full turn
9. Place a reference mark on the old belt at each sprocket alignment with white paint pen (helps if reusing temporarily, and confirms marks are correct).
10. Loosen the tensioner pulley bolt — allow spring tension to relax, then wedge it loose and secure with wire.
11. Slip the old timing belt off.
12. Remove the old water pump (3 bolts or 4 bolts, 10mm):
    - Have drain pan ready — remaining coolant will spill
    - Clean the mating surface thoroughly
    - Install new water pump with new gasket or RTV as specified
    - Torque water pump bolts to spec (typically 10–12 Nm)
13. Inspect and replace cam seals and crank seal if leaking.
14. Verify all timing marks are still at TDC.
15. Route new timing belt onto crank sprocket first, then idler, then intake cam, then over the water pump (if it routes over pump), then exhaust cam, keeping belt as tight as possible on the non-tensioner side.
16. Install the tensioner — allow it to apply tension to the belt.
17. **Verify all 3 timing marks are aligned with belt installed.**
18. Rotate the engine **2 full turns clockwise by hand** (via crank bolt).
19. Recheck all timing marks at TDC — all must be precisely aligned.
20. If marks are off: remove belt and start over — do not run with incorrect timing.
21. Tighten tensioner lock bolt to spec.
22. Reinstall timing belt covers.
23. Reinstall crank pulley — torque to spec.
24. Refill coolant, install drive belt.
25. Start engine and verify no knocking, correct idle, no warning lights.
26. Check for coolant leaks.

## 10. COMMON FAULT CODES — ENGINE

### P0016 / P0017 — Crankshaft/Camshaft Position Timing Correlation

**Description:** ECM detects that cam timing is not matching expected crank position — timing chain misalignment or excessive stretch.

**Causes:**
- Stretched timing chain
- Failed/weak chain tensioner
- Worn cam or crank sprocket
- Oil pressure too low (tensioner relies on oil pressure)
- Wrong cam timing after recent engine work

**Diagnosis:**
1. Check oil level and pressure first
2. Remove valve cover, check chain slack manually
3. Inspect cam timing marks at TDC
4. Check tensioner function

**Fix:**
- If chain stretched >1 tooth off: replace chain and tensioner assembly
- If oil starvation caused it: fix oil system, then inspect chain

---

### P0300 — Random Misfire Detected
### P0301 / P0302 / P0303 — Cylinder 1/2/3 Misfire

**Description:** ECM detects irregular crankshaft deceleration indicating misfire.

**Causes (1KR-FE, ranked):**
1. Fouled or worn spark plugs
2. Failed ignition coil (check resistance: primary 0.5–0.9 Ω, secondary 10–16 kΩ)
3. Oil in spark plug wells (valve cover gasket leak)
4. Fuel injector failure (clogged or dead)
5. Low compression (rings, valves)
6. Vacuum leak (lean misfire)

**Fix:**
1. Replace spark plugs (NGK ILKAR7L11, gap 1.0 mm, torque 18 Nm)
2. Test and replace ignition coil if failed
3. Replace valve cover gasket if oil in plug wells
4. Clean or replace injectors
5. Compression test to rule out mechanical issue

---

### P0171 — System Too Lean (Bank 1)

**Description:** O2 sensor feedback indicates air-fuel mixture is leaner than commanded — too much air or too little fuel.

**Causes (ranked):**
1. Vacuum leak (cracked intake hose, loose brake booster line)
2. Dirty/failed MAF sensor (under-reports airflow)
3. Clogged fuel injectors
4. Weak fuel pump (low pressure)
5. Failed O2 sensor (false reading)
6. Clogged air filter
7. EGR stuck open

**Fix:**
1. Inspect all vacuum and intake hoses — spray carb cleaner around joints with engine running to find leaks (idle change = leak)
2. Clean MAF sensor with MAF cleaner spray (do not touch wire)
3. Fuel pressure test: spec ~300–400 kPa at idle
4. Clean injectors with injector cleaner additive; replace if dead
5. Replace O2 sensor if >100k km

---

### P0340 — Camshaft Position Sensor Circuit Malfunction

**Description:** No signal or erratic signal from cam position sensor.

**Location:** CMP sensor is on the front of the cylinder head, near the intake cam sprocket, bolt-on with a single connector.

**Causes:**
1. Failed cam position sensor
2. Damaged sensor wiring (shorts, open circuit, chafing)
3. Reluctor ring damage on cam sprocket
4. Oil contamination of sensor air gap

**Fix:**
1. Check wiring for damage near the sensor
2. Measure sensor resistance (varies by sensor type — check spec)
3. Test signal with oscilloscope — should see square wave at cranking speed
4. Replace sensor (Toyota Part: 90919-05040 or equivalent, ~$30–$60)

---

### P1349 — VVT-i System Malfunction

**Description:** ECM commands VVT-i advance but cam timing does not respond or lags excessively.

**Causes:**
1. VVT-i solenoid clogged with sludge (most common)
2. VVT-i solenoid failed electrically
3. Oil sludge blocking VVT-i passages in cam sprocket
4. Low oil level or pressure
5. Wrong oil viscosity

**Fix:**
1. Check oil level and condition
2. Clean or replace VVT-i solenoid (see section 8)
3. Perform engine flush if sludged
4. If VVT sprocket is internally sludged: soak in engine flush, or replace sprocket assembly

---

### P0010 / P0011 — VVT-i Solenoid Control / Cam Position Over-Advanced

**P0010:** Circuit malfunction in cam OCV (Oil Control Valve) solenoid
**P0011:** Intake cam timing is over-advanced beyond the ECM command

**P0010 Fix:** Check solenoid wiring, measure resistance (6.9–7.9 Ω), replace if failed
**P0011 Fix:** Clean VVT-i solenoid screen, check oil pressure, check for sludge in VVT passages; replace solenoid if needed
